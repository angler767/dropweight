Physics of A Dropweight

  • Thin Black Bar represents Arm
  • Red Circle represents the Hinge
  • Rectangle attached to Black Bar Represents The Attachable Weight
  • White Dot represents the point at which the string is attached and the distance it travels is the length it is stretched
  • Yellow Circle and Green Rectangle represent the "gripper" that adjusts the tension on the string
  • Appearing White Circles represent the distance the string is stretched when the bar is lowered

The diagram on the right is that of a simplified dropweight stringing machine. The physics behind a dropweight stringing machine are simple. Tension is applied to the string when the weight bar is horizontal, as the end of the hover animation would suggest. The gripper is spun in a clockwise direction to adjust the tension in the string until the tension bar is at a horizontal. When the bar is at a horizontal and has no acceleration, the system is at equilibrium which means the sum of torques is zero and solving for the tension in the string is a simple statics question. The tension in the string multiplied by the radius of the hinge has to equal the sum of moment of inertia of the bar multiplied by the acceleration of gravity and the moment of inertia of the weight multiplied by the acceleration of gravity.

Stringing Tips for New Stringers (dropweight)

  • Prevent compression of frame by pushing top of post out and bottom of post in before tightening screws
  • DO NOT STRETCH FRAME WHEN MOUNTING ONTO POSTS TENSION FROM STRINGS WILL DAMAGE THE FRAME
  • When adjusting tension with gripper, only adjust when tension bar is vertical
  • General rule is to string horizontals from bottom to top
  • If hybrid stringing, thicker string used for verticals and thinner strings used for horizontals
  • Straighten horizontals after tensioning due to fly clamps pulling them down
  • 3.5 Armlengths for verticals and 3 armlengths for horizontals is a general rule if you're new
  • Double tension first 2 and last 2 horizontals with +2 tension
